About Me

My name is SeHee Han, and I am a Korean undergraduate student studying civil engineering at KONGJU NATIONAL UNIVERSITY. I enrolled in the university in 2017 and have been working as an undergraduate research assistant at the Geotechnical Engineering Laboratory since March 2022.

My interest in the environment has always been strong, but after learning about the crucial role of social infrastructure in ensuring public safety, I decided to pursue civil engineering. I am particularly intrigued by geotechnical engineering and am dedicated to learning as much as I can about this field through my studies.

One of my academic goals is to establish a Resonant Column Torsional Shear (RCTS) testing system using Python. Additionally, I have already passed the written exam for the Civil Engineering certification and am currently preparing for the practical exam. I am proud to have received an Outstanding Award at an academic conference for my research in capston design subject.

In my personal life, I enjoy watching educational YouTube videos and reading books. I also love spending time outdoors and exploring new places.

My ultimate aspiration is to publish a paper in a SCI or KCI-rated journal. I believe that my academic challenges have helped me grow as an individual, and I am excited to see where my future in civil engineering will lead me.
